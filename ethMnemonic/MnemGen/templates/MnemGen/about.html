About

Author: Daniel Mistrik

This project was inspired by the complexity of Ethereum addresses. The 42-character long hexadecimal is a combination of seemingly random numbers and letters making it incredibly difficult to transfer through word of mouth. Misspelling or forgetting a single character can mean disaster. While improvements, such as ENS have improved the situation they are not automatic to every address and often require payment to work. In short, the standard Ethereum address system is a large and complicated one that is not intuitive and difficult to use and share in-person.

Inspired by what3words which turned the similarly complicated system of geolocation into three simple words this project converts every ethereum address into a unique mnemonic. Every ethereum address is assigned a unique and permanent combination of 14 words. The mnemonic is composed of non-similar words so a change of even one character in the address should make a noticeably different mnemonic. One might ask why this mnemonic is 14 words but what3words is just 3! The simple answer is that for security reasons the number of possible ethereum addresses is far greater than the number of coordinates on Earth so this mnemonic needs to be larger.

This project supports EIP-55 and is case sensitive.
